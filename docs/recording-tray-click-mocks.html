Snapforge — Recording tray icon + click action

Eight design directions for the menu-bar pill shown while a screen recording is in progress. Each card pairs the resting pill (left, inside a mock menu bar) with its click-action dropdown (right, the NSMenu that appears on click). The side panel describes interaction semantics and what differs from today's pill (D1 baseline).

D1
Classic — dot · REC · time · pause · stop
Today's shipping pill. All controls inline. Click anywhere on the pill opens the dropdown; ⏸ and ■ glyphs hint at the menu items but are not directly clickable in the menu bar.

Click action

  • Single click → menu drops down anchored to pill.
  • Disabled ● Recording... header shows live state.
  • Pause/Stop swap with Resume when paused.

Notes

  • Widest of the eight (~110px). Slot-conflict risk on narrow notch displays.
D2
Minimal — pulsing dot + time
Strips REC text and inline controls. Relies on the dropdown for all actions. Saves ~50px of menu-bar space and reads as "just a timer".

Click action

  • Click ⏸ → pause/resume directly (no menu).
  • Click ■ → stop & save directly.
  • Click dot/time → dropdown with secondary actions only.

Notes

  • Requires multiple NSStatusItems or a custom view (NSStatusBarButton with regions).
  • Highest interaction efficiency. Highest implementation cost.
D7
Cassette — twin spinning reels + time
Skeuomorphic. Reels stop spinning when paused — instant visual feedback for state. Playful but unmistakable.
